About Me

I'm a PhD candidate at the University of Melbourne, Australia currently studying under the supervision of Christian Reichardt. I work as a member of the Simons Observatory collaboration with research focussing on (sub-)mm sources and transients.

I completed my BSc in Physics at the University of Melbourne in 2018 before taking a year off to travel the Americas where I spent just over a month at Cerro-Tololo Inter-American Observatory in La Serena, Chile assisting in the writing of a paper dedicated to galaxy cluster collisions. After returning home I went on to obtain my MSc also from the University of Melbourne in 2020.

Outside of astronomy and astrophysics I have had a long appreciation for film and screen, completing a three year stream of film making courses within my BSc. Complementary to this I worked at Hoyts cinemas between 2013 and 2021 where I built my experience in frontline customer experience, supervision and into management with roles including social media management, staff hiring and training and financial management.

Research Interests

I have covered a broad region of cosmology and associated areas throughout my work with the Simons Observatory collaboration. Beginning with beam calibration in my MSc, my PhD has been focussed firstly on Galactic Science and now I have moved into time domain science with the discovery and analysis of transients in the (sub-)mm. I enjoy exploring the intersections of observational cosmology with high-energy, time-domain astrophysics.
Below are some research highlights, you can find a full list of publications here.

2023

Polarization Fraction of PGCCs

In this work we perform a stacking analysis of Planck Galactic cold clumps. We were able to measure, for the first time, the mean-squared and RMS polarization fraction of Galactic cold clumps. Tests were also performed on the relationship between this polarization contribution across the Galactic dust to cold clump properties such a their flux density, luminosity and Galactic distribution where we found no strong evidence of these affecting the polarization and hence likely the magnetic fields of these objects. We also performed a forecast for observing Galactic cold clumps in intensity and polarization with the Simons Observatory.

2023

[Depreciated Software] SO LAT Primitive Beams

The Simons Observatory in it's early stages of LAT development required extensive analysis of the beam functions for future calibration efforts. For my MSc project, I explored measuring the beam function, approximating a circular Gaussian beam, by scanning point sources. Furthermore I performed a Fisher forecast on the beam function covariance to see how point source calibration would impact cosmological parameters.
