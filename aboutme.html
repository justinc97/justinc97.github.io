Hey I'm Justin!

I'm a PhD candidate working under the supervision of Christian Reichardt at The University of Melbourne.
I completed my BSc in Physics at the University of Melbourne in 2018 before taking a year off to travel the Americas where I spent just over a month at Cerro-Tololo Inter-American Observatory in La Serena, Chile.
After returning home I went on to obtain my MSc also from the University of Melbourne in 2020, and begin my PhD the following year.

Travelling

Like most in the astro field, I absolutely love to travel, especially when it's for a conference in a warm part of the world!
I think it's incredibly important to visit all parts of the world and experience all kinds of cultures, and I'm very privelaged to be part of a community that puts focus on this (and builds experiments in some pretty unique places).

Film & TV

This might seem pretty generic to say that I love watching movies and TV but my passions here delve a little deeper.
I actually studied quite a bit of film making in my high school and undergraduate degree. I also have some family heavily involved in the industry.
Oh, and I worked at a cinema for 10 years! Working my way from sweeping popcorn off seats to management.
Safe to say, if I wasn't so passionate for science, I'd probably be in quite a different field...

Sports, Hiking & Camping

Most nights of the week that I'm not still in the office or binging a new series, I'm probably playing sport.
I've always loved sports and throughout the years I've certainly tried as many as I could. Tennis, futsal, basketball, volleyball (and rebound beach volleyball) and netball to name the few I put the most time into. Lately I've pulled myself back to just volleyball and netball (with the odd week rebound beach volleyball - if you haven't tried it, do yourself a favour and check it out).

Most weekends that aren't already booked out, my partner and I are looking for somewhere new to go for an adventure. We are avid hikers and have no shortage of great, off the beaten track, places to explore.
Coming from farming families, we're no strangers to 'bush bashing' as we'd say and camping out under the stars.
